Frequently Asked Questions

What electrical calculations can WattsWise perform?

WattsWise performs voltage drop calculations, power analysis (P, Q, S, PF), insulation testing (DAR/PI), current unbalance analysis, short circuit calculations, energy consumption calculations, and cable size conversions (AWG ↔ mm²).

Is WattsWise suitable for professional electricians and electrical engineers?

Absolutely. WattsWise is designed specifically for electrical engineers, master electricians, and electrical contractors who need accurate, reliable calculations for commercial and residential projects. It follows industry standards with optional normative guidance.

How accurate are the voltage drop calculations?

WattsWise uses the Constant K Method for voltage drop calculations, providing precise ΔV% and ΔV (Volts) results. The app supports both single-phase and three-phase circuits.

What is insulation testing and how does it work in the app?

Insulation testing helps diagnose the condition of electrical insulation. The PRO version calculates DAR (R60s/R30s), PI (R10min/R1min), and temperature-corrected resistance per IEEE 43 standards, providing clear verdicts like "Good" or "Questionable".

Does the app support different electrical standards?

WattsWise is designed to be agnostic to electrical standards. While it provides optional normative suggestions (NEC/IEC), the core calculations use universal electrical formulas, making it suitable for professionals worldwide regardless of local codes.

Is there a free version of WattsWise available?

Yes, WattsWise offers a free version with core electrical calculation features including voltage drop, power analysis, and short circuit calculations. The PRO version includes advanced diagnostic tools like insulation testing, current unbalance analysis, and energy consumption calculations.
